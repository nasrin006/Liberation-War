Vertical Timeline Section

  • 7 March 1971
    On March 7, 1971, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman delivered a historic speech in Dhaka that is considered a de facto declaration of Bangladesh's independence. Before a crowd of over a million people, amidst escalating tensions with West Pakistan's military rulers, Mujibur Rahman tactically avoided a direct declaration of independence to prevent immediate military retaliation, instead issuing a clear call for a non-cooperation movement and preparation for a potential war.
  • 26 March 1971
    On 26 March 1971, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman declared the independence of Bangladesh, marking the formal beginning of the Bangladesh Liberation War. This declaration came in the early hours of the morning, shortly after the Pakistani military launched a brutal crackdown on the civilian population of East Pakistan, known as "Operation Searchlight".
  • 16 December 1971
    December 16, 1971, is a pivotal date in South Asian history, marking the end of the nine-month-long Bangladesh Liberation War and the birth of an independent People's Republic of Bangladesh. This day, celebrated as Victory Day (Bijoy Dibos), commemorates the ultimate triumph of freedom over oppression after a brutal conflict characterized by widespread atrocities and genocide.
