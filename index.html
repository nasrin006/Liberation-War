1971 Liberation War

A historic struggle for freedom, dignity, and independence.

Brief History

The 1971 Liberation War was Bangladesh's bloody struggle for independence from Pakistan, sparked by political disenfranchisement and cultural suppression, leading to genocide by the Pakistani Army (Operation Searchlight) and a massive refugee crisis, ultimately resulting in India's intervention, the Pakistani surrender on December 16, 1971, and the birth of Bangladesh as a sovereign nation, a pivotal moment forging its national identity.
